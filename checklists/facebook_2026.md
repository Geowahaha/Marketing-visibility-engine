# Facebook / Social Reach Playbook — 2026 ("Andromeda" era)

This is the human-side companion to the technical auditor. The code can't post
for you, but these are the levers that actually move organic reach in 2026,
based on the current state of Meta's algorithm. Work top-down.

## The one thing to internalise
Facebook's feed is now a **discovery engine**, not a friends-and-family feed.
Up to ~50% of an average feed is recommended content from accounts the user
does **not** follow. That means a small page *can* reach far beyond its follower
count — if the content earns it. Average organic reach sits around 1–2% of
followers; that's normal, not a penalty. You grow by winning **discovery reach**,
not broadcast reach.

## Ranking signals that matter most (highest → lowest leverage)
1. **Saves & shares** — the strongest signals. One share-to-Story or save is
   worth more than dozens of likes. Make content people want to *keep* or *send*
   to someone (price lists, before/after, "how it's made", spec guides).
2. **Watch time & completion rate (Reels)** — all videos are now Reels. Short,
   vertical, hook in the first 1–2 seconds. This is the fastest discovery lever.
3. **Meaningful comments** — the algorithm now judges comment *quality*, not
   count. Replies beyond emojis/one-word. Ask a real question in the caption;
   reply to every comment within the first hour.
4. **Dwell time** — how long someone lingers. Carousels and "read-on" captions
   that make people stop scrolling.
5. **Intent signals** — someone searching your brand after seeing a post is a
   huge positive. Branded queries are one area that *benefits* in the AI era.

## Account-level rules (these gate everything above)
- **Pick 2 core themes and stay on them.** The algorithm reads your last ~9–12
  posts to assign your page a "topic tag." For Suphan Casting that's e.g.
  *(1) the casting process / craftsmanship* and *(2) finished products + use
  cases*. Don't dilute with off-topic posts.
- **Originality.** Native, original content beats reposts/reshares. Upload video
  directly to Facebook (native), never link out to YouTube in the post body.
- **Consistency over volume.** ~3–5 quality posts/week beats daily filler.
  More than ~2 posts/day can get flagged as noise and *lower* per-post reach.
- **Don't post a bare external link.** Put the link in the first comment or use
  the link sticker; link-only posts are throttled.

## Facebook as a search surface (Facebook SEO)
- Fill the Page out completely: category, full "About", address, hours, phone,
  website, services. A complete Page ranks in both Facebook search *and* Google.
- Use real keywords people type ("โรงหล่อทราย", "รับหล่อทองเหลือง",
  "sand casting Thailand") in the Page name area, About, and post captions.
- Public Pages/posts can appear in Google results — another discovery channel.

## Content engine for a casting factory (concrete)
- **Process Reels:** molten pour, mould-making, finishing. High completion +
  shareable. This is your single best format.
- **Before/after carousels:** raw cast → machined/polished part. High saves.
- **Spec/price reference posts:** "metals we cast + typical tolerances" — saved
  and re-shared by buyers. Pair with a clear CTA to the website contact form.
- **Customer/job spotlights:** builds the entity/brand signal AI engines also
  reward (YouTube + branded web mentions correlate with AI visibility).

## Cross-channel flywheel (ties back to the website auditor)
AI-search visibility correlates strongly with **YouTube presence** and
**branded web mentions across multiple domains**. So:
1. Post the process video natively on Facebook **and** as a YouTube Short.
2. Make sure the website has the matching `Organization`/`LocalBusiness` schema
   with `sameAs` pointing to the Facebook + YouTube URLs (the generator does
   this). That triangulation is what lets Claude/ChatGPT/Gemini connect the
   brand entity across sources and cite you.
3. Keep NAP (Name/Address/Phone) **identical** on the site, Facebook, and Google
   Business Profile. Inconsistency breaks the entity and kills local + AI trust.

## What to ignore
- Buying followers / engagement pods — Andromeda discounts low-quality
  interaction and it can suppress reach.
- Like-bait ("comment YES") — the comment-*quality* model devalues it.
- Over-posting to "stay visible" — it does the opposite.
